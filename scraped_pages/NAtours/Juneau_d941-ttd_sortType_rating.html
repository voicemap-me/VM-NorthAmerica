With no roads in or out, Juneau might seem a strange choice for a state capital—but it’s certainly no strange choice for travelers looking to experience the 49th state. From this town hanging off a mountainside, there’s nowhere to go but into the wild: Juneau tours lead to the foot of glaciers, the playgrounds of whales, the edges of the forest, and beyond. Of course, gold-rush history and Alaskan cuisine shouldn’t be overlooked, and there will be plenty of time for that between whale-watching, hiking, paddling, and getting familiar with America’s wild side.

Q:What are the best excursions in Juneau?
A:

There are plenty of high-quality Juneau excursions to choose from. Looking at ratings and reviews by previous customers, these are the best tours available right now:

  • Juneau Wildlife Whale Watching

Q:Are guided excursions in Juneau worth it?
A:

Yes, guided excursions in Juneau are worth it. Expert guides offer in-depth knowledge about the area’s history, wildlife, and stunning natural landscapes. Whether you’re exploring glaciers, whale watching, or touring the city, a guided excursion ensures you make the most of your visit to this incredible part of the world.

Q:How much do Juneau tours cost?
A:

A typical price range for the most popular whale-watching and glacier tours is US$180-$360, but Juneau excursion prices vary depending on the type and length of the tour. For example, you can join a scavenger hunt tour around the city for around US$20, while a private land and sea tour with whale watching and a visit to Mendenhall Glacier costs around US$3,500.

Q:What activities are included on Juneau excursions?
A:

Juneau excursions let you experience the natural beauty and cultural heritage of the city, and each tour differs in terms of inclusions. Typical activities include whale watching, glacier trekking, dogsledding, and kayaking, while other tours let you enjoy helicopter rides, walks through the historical city, and wildlife spotting.

Q:How many days in Juneau is enough?
A:

A stay of 3–4 days in Juneau is typically enough to explore the main attractions and enjoy some excursions into the surrounding nature. This allows time to experience glacier tours, whale watching, and the city’s highlights without feeling rushed.

Q:What is the best month to visit Juneau?
A:

The best time to visit Juneau is between May and August, which coincides with the humpback whales’ migration to Alaska. The days are also warm, long, and sunny, making it ideal for other types of outdoor excursions—think glacier hikes, e-bike tours, and helicopter rides over epic ice fields.
