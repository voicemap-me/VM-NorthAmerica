Grand Canyon National Park is one of the most popular national parks in the country, drawing more than 4 million visitors each year. Most tourists will walk to the South Rim, take a few pictures, and move on. There are, however, dozens of Grand Canyon National Park tours that offer a different perspective on the massive canyon. Get a big-picture aerial view with a plane or helicopter ride, or go deeper with a hike into the canyon or a boat trip down the river that has been carving its way through the rock for millions of years.

Q:What is the best month to go to the Grand Canyon?
A:

To avoid huge crowds and hot temperatures, visit the Grand Canyon between March and May or September and November. These shoulder seasons are just as beautiful as the summer, but they have more comfortable temperatures. Most Grand Canyon tours are still offered during these months, and park facilities are open.

Q:Is a guided tour of the Grand Canyon worth it?
A:

Yes, whether they involve a bus, raft, plane, or helicopter, guided Grand Canyon tours are always worth it. These tours offer once-in-a-lifetime experiences and may include exclusive access to places you couldn’t visit alone. Plus, you’ll probably learn more about the park with a guide.

Q:How many days do you really need for the Grand Canyon?
A:

One day is enough if you just want to see the views and take photos along the South Rim. If you want to fit in hiking or book one of the many Grand Canyon tours available, aim for 2–3 days. Rafting the entire canyon, meanwhile, takes around one week.

Q:What is the prettiest side of the Grand Canyon?
A:

The Grand Canyon’s three rims are all pretty in their own way, but the South Rim gives you the world-famous views typically seen in movies and magazines. Mather Point, a popular place for sunrise and sunset pictures, is among the prettiest viewpoints along the South Rim.

Q:When should you not visit the Grand Canyon?
A:

If you’re worried about traffic, high temperatures, and limited availability for lodging, do not visit the Grand Canyon during the summer. In particular, avoid the busiest months of July and August. If you want to see the North Rim, don’t visit in the winter when roads and facilities are closed.
