While you might be tempted to spend all your time in the Riviera Maya and the Yucatan on the beach, the region offers much more than white sand and warm water. Riviera Maya and Yucatan tours reveal a different side of the Mexican state by showcasing Mayan ruins, secret cenotes, and colonial towns. Some excursions whisk you to a world wonder or sail to snorkeling hot spots in the Caribbean Sea, while others focus on exploring the jungle in an ATV or tasting local delights such as tacos and tequilas.

A:

The best tours in the Riviera Maya combine nature and relaxation. According to customer reviews, the highest-rated options right now include:

  • Chichen Itza, Cenote and Valladolid All-Inclusive Tour

  • Chichen Itza Early Access Tour, Cenote Swim, Tequila and Lunch
Q:Are guided tours in the Riviera Maya worth it?
A:

Yes, Riviera Maya excursions provide great value and special benefits you may not get on your own. Reserve early access to Chichen Itza, entry to exclusive beach clubs, and all-inclusive half-day tours with entry fees included. Add to that local expertise, easy transport, and knowledgeable guides.

Q:How much do the Riviera Maya tours cost?
A:

Riviera Maya excursions exist for all price points, from inexpensive transport to the airport or tourist sites all the way to epic multi-day adventures that take in the best of the entire Yucatan. Those who prefer to explore Maya masterpieces in seclusion can make use of self-guided tours, while others can book private group tours that also pick you up and drop you off at your resortthe choice is yours.

Q:Can you go off your resort in the Riviera Maya?
A:

Yes, resorts allow you to venture off the resort grounds to explore the archeological, cultural, and festive offerings of the region. Just be sure that you know what rules there are (if any) for returning at night, and always travel with a form of identification.

Q:What activities are included in Riviera Maya tours?
A:

Riviera Maya excursions offer a variety of activities for all travelers. Consider a sailboat tour to Isla Mujeres, an all-day all-inclusive party at one of the areas premier clubs, or a trip to see Chichen Itza and the areas famous cenotes. If you can think it up, theres an excursion in the Yucatan for you.

Q:Are mosquitoes bad in the Riviera Maya?
A:

The quick answer is that it depends. Mosquitos love hot and humid weather, which describes the climate of the Riviera Maya in the summer months. The abundance of bugs depends on several factors, including the presence of stagnant water. Most resorts have mitigation strategies, and you can choose lodging thats closer to the beach for a higher chance of a steady wind.
