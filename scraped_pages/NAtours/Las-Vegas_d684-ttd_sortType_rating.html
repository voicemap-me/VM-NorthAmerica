Q:Are guided tours in Las Vegas worth it?
A:

Yes, guided Las Vegas tours are worth it. You’ll get to experience the city beyond your resort or casino, learning about its 120-year history as one of America’s favorite nightlife destinations. Plus, many guided tours offer easy transport to iconic sites like the Grand Canyon and Hoover Dam.

Q:How much do Las Vegas tours cost?
A:

Sightseeing tours around the city of Las Vegas start at US$55 per person. Day excursions, such as tours of the Grand Canyon and ATV driving in the Mojave Desert, range for US$70–US$170, depending on the duration of the tour and whether or not meals and hotel pickup are included.

Q:What do you see on Las Vegas tours?
A:

Las Vegas tours take you past the city’s top attractions, from the High Roller and Fountains of Bellagio on the Strip to the Mob Museum and old-school casinos on Fremont Street. (Or you can hop on a helicopter to see everything at once.) Day tours outside of the city are also popular—you can visit the Grand Canyon or Hoover Dam in the morning and be back at your resort in time for dinner and a show.

Q:How many days in Las Vegas is enough?
A:

Plan to spend three days in Las Vegas. Dedicate the first day to gambling and enjoying the pool and amenities at your resort or casino, then use the next day to visit museums and attractions, see a game or live show, and hop on the High Roller. A third day gives you enough time for a day trip to nearby sites like Red Rock Canyon.

Q:What is the best month to visit Las Vegas?
A:

A desert city, Las Vegas frequently reaches temperatures above 100°F in the summer months, so you’ll be most comfortable if you visit from March–May or September–November. For the best hotel rates, try to plan your trip outside of major holidays or events, such as the Super Bowl or conventions and tradeshows.
