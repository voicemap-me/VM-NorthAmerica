Thanks to its prime position on the shores of the Caribbean Sea, Cozumel is a hot spot for snorkeling and scuba diving. It’ll come as no surprise, then, that Cozumel tours showcase the underwater wonderlands surrounding the Mexican island. Sailing trips take you to prime snorkel and diving spots inaccessible by land, and submarine and sea helmet adventures increase your chances of spotting sea turtles, starfish, and stingrays. If you’d rather stay dry, ATV and Jeep excursions take you off the beaten path to jade-green cenotes and limestone caverns.

Q:What are the best tours in Cozumel?
A:

There are plenty of high-quality tours in Cozumel. Looking at ratings and reviews previously given by customers, these are the best tours available right now:

  • Mr. Sanchos Beach Club All-Inclusive Day Pass

  • Cozumel Food Tour

Q:How much do Cozumel tours cost?
A:

That depends. If you book an all-inclusive day pass to local beach clubs, you can expect to pay US$50 to US$75 a person, but you can also find Cozumel excursions for as low as US$25 and private boat excursions that will reach upward of US$170 a person depending on the size of your group.

Q:What activities are included on Cozumel excursions?
A:

Most Cozumel excursions include exploring the island's beautiful waterways. From sailing on catamarans and snorkeling the El Cielo Reefs to ATV rides that take you through the jungle discovering hidden caverns, there’s a full range of experiences depending on your mood and budget.

Q:Can I explore Cozumel on my own?
A:

Yes, you can absolutely explore Cozumel on your own. However, many travelers enjoy an excursion while they’re on the island. Whether it's an all-inclusive day pass to check out a popular beach club, a food or snorkeling tour, or a private boat trip, you can easily find an experience that will enhance your trip.

Q:How many days in Cozumel is enough?
A:

Three to five days will allow you to relax and explore the island beyond the perfect beaches, free-flowing drinks, and endless taco trucks. If you’re looking for more than just relaxation, spend the extra days discovering ancient Maya ruins, ziplining through the dense jungle, or exploring hidden cenotes by ATV.

Q:What’s so special about Cozumel?
A:

On the surface, Cozumel has stunning beaches, candy-colored reefs, epic snorkeling, and a laid-back party vibe. But there's more to the island than meets the eye, which makes it so special. You can ride jungle trails, fly through the treetops on ziplines, and discover ancient Mayan ruins—all in one incredible destination.
