Q:Are the Tulum ruins worth seeing?
A:

Yes, the Tulum ruins are definitely worth visiting—touring the ruins is considered one of the best activities in the area. The structures here, like the Temple of the Frescoes and the Castillo, are well preserved, and knowledgeable tour guides are on hand to help you delve into the history and culture of the site.

Q:How much do Tulum tours cost?
A:

There are Tulum tours for every budget, with a range of price points available. On the lower end of the spectrum, guided walking tours run about US$20, while luxury day trips can cost as much as US$4,000 for your group. Whether you are looking for high-end experiences or are sticking to a tight budget, there is something for everyone.

Q:How long are guided tours of Tulum?
A:

That depends on which tour you choose. Some tours, including the popular options that visit Chichen Itza and a cenote, may last about 3–4 hours. There are shorter walking tours and extensive day-long tours that combine multiple experiences or entail a full-day boat trip.

Q:What activities are included on Tulum tours?
A:

There is a slew of different activities offered on Tulum tours. Visit the ancient Mayan ruins, which are perched on cliffs above the Caribbean Sea, is a top choice. Snorkeling and swimming opportunities also abound, including in the region’s famous cenotes, which are dramatic sinkholes filled with water.

Q:How many days should I spend in Tulum?
A:

That depends on your itinerary and interests. On the shorter end, three or four days is sufficient to visit the area’s archaeological sites and cenotes, experience the local culture, and enjoy beach and water activities. A weeklong trip would allow more time to explore or just relax by the water.
