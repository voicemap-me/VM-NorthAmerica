Big, bold, and brash, Miami can feel overwhelming to visitors. So let expert-led Miami tours lead the way. From the palm-fringed paradise that is Miami's coastline to the music-filled streets of Little Havana, this dynamic city beckons travelers with its fusion of diverse cultures, legendary nightlife scene, and tantalizing flavors. You can find Miami tours that showcase South Beach’s art deco architecture and the colorful street art scene of Wynwood, as well as tours that will take you out onto the ocean by yacht or jet ski or even far off the beaten path in an ATV.

Q:What are the best tours in Miami?

There are plenty of highly rated Miami tours. According to ratings and reviews previously given by customers, these are the best tours available right now: 

  • Little Havana Food and Walking Tour in Miami

Q:Are guided tours in Miami worth it?
A:

Yes, taking a guided tour in Miami is worth it. An expert tour guide can enhance your visit by providing insights, convenience, safety, and access to experiences that might not otherwise be available. Miami tours allow for a deeper understanding of the city’s attractions, historic sites, and culture.

Q:How much do Miami tours cost?
A:

Miami tours are highly variable in price. Walking tours are typically priced on the lower end of the spectrum, while luxury, private tours can command a much higher price. Whether you are setting aside US$20 or US$2,000, there is something for every budget in Miami.

Q:Can you tour the port of Miami?
A:

Miami Cruise Port (also known as PortMiami) is a busy place, as it welcomes vessels from across the world. There aren’t any cruises of the port specifically; most travelers simply use it as a jumping-off point to explore the city. Located near downtown Miami on Dodge Island, PortMiami offers easy access to shore excursions, city exploration, and picturesque views of Miami's skyline.

Q:What can you see on Miami tours?
A:

There is a ton of variety when it comes to Miami’s tour options. Popular picks include city sightseeing tours, which take you past popular spots like Little Havana, Vizcaya Museum and Gardens, and Bayside Marketplace. You can also take a boat tour through Biscayne Bay or opt for adventurous activities such as an airboat ride through the Everglades.

Q:How many days in Miami is enough?
A:

Budgeting four or five days is ideal to achieve a well-rounded visit to Miami. This time frame allows you to explore the city’s many attractions, including its famous beaches and fascinating neighborhoods. You’ll have time to delve into the cultural and culinary scene and have ample time to take a day trip or two to areas like the Florida Keys or the Everglades.
