From its world-famous cities to its national parks—and from sea to shining sea—the United States offers more opportunities for adventure than you could experience in a lifetime. But you can at least make a good start with these US tours. If your tastes skew more metropolitan, admire the monuments of Washington DC, gawk at the skyscrapers of New York, and cruise along the waterways of Chicago. For a trip into nature, make a pilgrimage to the Grand Canyon or go snorkeling in Hawaii. Whether you’re whale watching in Alaska or taking steamboat rides on the Mississippi, this continent-spanning country is your oyster.

Q:What are the best tours in the United States?
A:

When you consider just how enormous the United States is—and how popular a tourist destination it is—you won’t be surprised that there are tens of thousands of tour options to choose from. At the moment, the top-rated USA tours by previous travelers (which is just the tip of the proverbial iceberg) are:

  • Grand Circle Island and Haleiwa 9 Hour Tour
  • Chicago Architecture River Cruise
  • New Orleans Ghost, Voodoo and Vampire Combo Tour
  • New York in One Day Guided Sightseeing Tour
  • Statue of Liberty and Ellis Island Tour: All Options
Q:Are guided tours in the US worth it?
A:

Yes, USA tours are absolutely worth it. Given how big the country is, it may seem daunting to try to figure out where to go and what to include in an itinerary. Going on a tour means someone else is navigating from place to place so you don’t have to. Tours enable you to see and do more during your vacation than you might be able to on your own, too, especially when it can be hard to narrow down your options.

Q:How much do USA tours cost?
A:

You probably won’t be surprised to learn that, with so many thousands of tour options in the United States, the price range is as huge as the country itself. There are guided walks of cities and neighborhoods that are US$10–20 per person, all the way up to private helicopter rides or yacht charters for US$10,000 and up per group. Most of the country’s top-rated tours are between US$30–100 per person, so no matter where you go you’re likely to find tour options that suit your budget.

Q:What do you see on USA tours?
A:

The options for things you can see and do throughout the country with US tours is nearly limitless. You can visit the Statue of Liberty in New York, ride an all-terrain vehicle in the desert outside Las Vegas, explore the Cuban neighborhood of Miami, zipline over a Hawaiian jungle, wander through the historic French Quarter in New Orleans, marvel at the expanse of the Grand Canyon, and so much more.

Q:How many days in the US is enough?
A:

The appropriate duration for a US vacation varies considerably, depending on what you want to see and do. If you’re visiting New York City, you should plan to stay for at least 5–7 days in order to see the main sights. For a visit to San Francisco, on the other hand, you may only need three days. Road trippers meandering along Route 66 from one end to the other should allow at least 10 days for a drive that includes plenty of time to stop and see the many roadside attractions. A cross-country road trip, though, is best if you’ve got at least three weeks to spare.

Q:What is the best month to visit the United States?
A:

In most of the US, the best time to visit is the summer high season, roughly June–August. That’s when the weather is reliably warm and there are often big festivals and events dotting the calendar. But the best time to visit Alaska (from May or July through August, depending on the area) is very different from the best time to visit Texas (the shoulder seasons of spring and autumn in some places, the winter months in others) so your best bet is to look at the recommendations for the specific states, regions, or cities you’ll be visiting.
