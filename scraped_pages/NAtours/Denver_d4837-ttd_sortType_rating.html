Q:What are the best tours in Denver?
A:

Colorado’s Mile High City is full of exciting tours for visitors. These are the top-rated Denver excursions available right now:

  • Discover Rocky Mountain National Park from Denver or Boulder
  • Small Group Tour of Pikes Peak and the Garden of the Gods from Denver
  • Half Day Rocky Mountain Escape
  • Downtown Denver Food Tour
  • Denver, Red Rocks, and Beyond
Q:How much do Denver tours cost?
A:

Self-guided tours, scavenger hunts, museum admission, and even Rockies baseball tickets can cost US$20 or less in the Mile High City. Pub crawls, half-day sightseeing tours, and hiking excursions in the nearby mountains might cost between $50 and $100 per person. Private tours with your own group of family and friends can cost $500 or more.

Q:What do you see on Denver tours?
A:

Many Denver tours focus on food and drink, featuring tastings at some of the city’s best restaurants, microbreweries, and distilleries. Neighborhood walking tours are a great way to admire Denver’s historic architecture, art galleries, and landmark buildings. Many Denver tours also take visitors outside the city limits to view stunning red-rock formations, looming snow-covered mountains, and high-alpine lakes.

Q:How many days in Denver is enough?
A:

Three days in Denver will give you enough time to explore the city’s neighborhoods, enjoy the excellent restaurants and upbeat bars, and see some key landmarks, such as downtown’s Union Station and the Capitol Building. You’ll also have time to watch a show at the Buell Theatre or attend a sporting event in this fan-friendly city—Denver is home to professional baseball, hockey, football, soccer, and lacrosse teams.

Q:What is the best month to visit Denver?
A:

September is the best month to visit Denver. In the late summer and early fall, arid and sunny days bring an average high temperature of 82°F (28°C) and cooler nights might be around 50°F (10°C). September is also a great month to visit since kids are back to school, so there are fewer families (and people in general) at key attractions and on local trails.

Q:What is the #1 attraction in Denver?
A:

According to past travelers, the #1 attraction in Denver is Red Rocks Park and Amphitheatre. The world-class concert venue just outside city limits is also home to a myriad of hiking trails amid dramatic red-rock scenery. It’s a must-visit when in the Mile High City.
