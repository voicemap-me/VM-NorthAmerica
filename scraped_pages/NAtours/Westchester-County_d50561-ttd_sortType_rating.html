Private Tour of The Armour-Stiner Octagon House in New York

The Armour-Stiner Octagon House was built in 1860 as a summer home. To this day, the Spring months here in The Hudson Valley are the most delightful time of the year. Victorian Springtime at The Octagon House, a one-hour tour, includes both a guided exploration of the lush 19th-century grounds and an unforgettable interior tour of this marvelously preserved Victorian Summer Home. Guests will see and smell a vibrant array of Victorian flowers inside the fully restored Lord and Burnham greenhouse, listen to an orchestra of songbirds in the Foxglove Garden, and step back in time with a stroll around the 360-degree verandah. In the interior portion of the tour, see many examples of how the natural world heavily influenced Victorian life and design.

Peekskill New York Culture and Food Walking Tour

Peekskill is one of New York's best-kept secrets and one of the oldest societies in America, dating back to the 1600s. Peekskill is home to an artist community, vintage bookstores, festivals, live music, and a huge foodie culture. We will visit bars, bakeries, dessert parlors, and cafes, as well as artist lofts, street art, boutiques, and bookstores. Your tour includes a combination of 10-15 food and non-food indoor and outdoor stops. Participants will enjoy bite-sized food, desserts, coffee, and alcoholic beverages, varying between Latino, Carib, Southern, and American culture. All food and drinks are included. More than just food and drinks, you will learn about Peekskill's rich history spanning 500 years through random, fun, and surprising facts! This will be a 2-mile walk with minimal seating or tables on uneven pavement. The tour starts at 11:30 am and ends at 1:30 pm. The tour is rain or shine. Please come with comfy shoes, an empty tummy, ready to mingle, and we'll do the rest.
