Ketchikan is a wilderness lover’s paradise. Close to downtown, a local guide can take you to see the region’s best black sand beaches, Native American totem pole parks, and viewpoints for black bears and spawning salmon. The waters around Tongass National Forest are preferred for kayaking among orcas, seals, and humpback whales, but Ketchikan excursions don’t stop there. Fish for Alaskan salmon and halibut, bike or zipline through lush rainforest, or take a plane ride over the spectacular mountains, waterfalls, and alpine lakes of Misty Fjords National Monument.

Q:What are the best tours in Ketchikan?

Q:How much do Ketchikan tours cost?
A:

Most Ketchikan excursions average US$85 per person and typically include a visit to Saxman Native Village, a historical city tour, or a stop at Rotary Beach to look for wildlife. Longer salmon-fishing excursions, ziplining adventures, and kayaking tours cost US$150 and up per person.

Q:How many days in Ketchikan is enough?
A:

While having three days or more in Ketchikan is ideal, you can glimpse this Alaskan city in a day or two. As it is the Salmon Capital of the World, you'll want to try your luck at salmon fishing when you are in Ketchikan. Another must-do activity is to learn about the area's rich Native American culture at the Totem Heritage Center and Saxman Native Village.

Q:What is the best month to visit Ketchikan?
A:

The summer months of May through September are the most popular time to visit Ketchikan. Salmon fishing and bear watching are popular activities during this time of year. Even though these months have the best weather, you should still plan on bringing rain gear as Ketchikan gets quite a bit of rain, even in the summer.

Q:Can the northern lights be seen in Ketchikan?
A:

Due to its location in southeastern Alaska, Ketchikan rarely gets the northern lights. The best time of year to catch a glimpse of this celestial phenomenon in Ketchikan is during the winter when there are more hours of darkness.

Q:Can you see bears in Ketchikan?
A:

Yes, Ketchikan is located in the Tongass National Forest, home to a population of black bears and some brown bears. Bears can be seen near Ketchikan, especially when the salmon are spawning. To have a better chance of seeing these fantastic creatures, you can book a tour that will take you to popular bear-viewing areas.
