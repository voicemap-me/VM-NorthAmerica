Mixing Caribbean shores with ancient Maya culture, Costa Maya offers equal opportunities for sightseeing and soaking up the sun. Costa Maya excursions transport you deep into the jungle to the 3,000-year-old Chacchoben Ruins to learn about one of the Maya empire’s most important ceremonial centers. Other tours whisk you into the water at the Bacalar Lagoon—known as the Lake of Seven Colors, thanks to its iridescent hues—and luxury beach clubs where you can enjoy massages and mojitos by the Caribbean Sea.

Q:Are guided Costa Maya tours worth it?
A:

Yes, most people find that guided Costa Maya tours are worth it. Traveling through the area with a guide helps eliminate the hassle of planning your own excursions and navigating an unfamiliar area. Plus, Costa Maya guided tours are educational, ensuring you learn the history of archeological sites like Chacchoben.

Q:How much do Costa Maya tours cost?
A:

Most Costa Maya tours cost between US$60 and US$150 for scuba diving trips or visits to top sites like the Mayan ruins of Chacchoben and the beautiful Bacalar Lagoon. The price variations reflect things like duration (full-day trips cost slightly more) and amenities like lunch or a private tour.

Q:What activities are included on Costa Maya excursions?
A:

Costa Maya excursions primarily revolve around the top activities in the area: visits to Chacchoben and Bacalar, or scuba diving trips. There are also options to take a cooking class, tour the area by electric bike, relax on a private beach, or go birdwatching with a guide.

Q:How many days in the Costa Maya is enough?
A:

The ideal number of days to spend in Costa Maya is 3–4 days. This duration allows time to explore the beaches, visit the Chacchoben Mayan ruins, enjoy water activities like snorkeling and diving, and experience the local culture and cuisine.

Q:What is the best month to visit the Costa Maya?
A:

March is the best month to visit Costa Maya. The weather is typically warm—but not sweltering—and dry, making it ideal for beach activities and exploring Mayan ruins. By visiting in March, you’ll avoid the headaches and potential dangers of the hurricane season, and you’ll miss the peak winter crowds.
